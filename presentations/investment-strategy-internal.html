Open Question Addressed

No legal barrier to integrating these systems.
Others are already doing it — not at our scale or depth.

Why It's Legally Safe

RESPA: Prohibits kickbacks between affiliated businesses — NOT analytics or visibility across systems.

Mortgage data (ECOA/HMDA): Regulates fair lending decisions. We don't make lending decisions — we read status data.

Affiliated mortgage arms: A builder knowing "plumbing is 3 weeks late" doesn't violate lending regulations. That's operational intelligence, not a loan decision.

Our design: Read-only APIs. Source systems don't know we're correlating. No write-back.

Open Question Addressed

"We tell you there's a problem before you know you have a problem."

How does the system flag a delay before BuildPro marks it late?

1. Comparative Neighborhood Metrics

Homes in the same community follow similar sequences. If homes 1–15 started plumbing by day 45 and home 16 hasn't by day 52 — something is wrong, even if no one marked it "late" yet.

Baseline = the builder's own historical data.

2. Signal Velocity (Silence = Danger)

We track data updates per deal per week across all 5 systems. A healthy deal: 10–20 signals/week. A deal that drops to 0 for 7+ days is dying quietly.

Our #1 predictive feature. More predictive than % complete.

3. Cross-System Sync Detection

Construction at 70% but mortgage at 20%? The house finishes before the loan is ready — closing slips.

No single-system tool can detect this. Unique to our architecture.

4. Explainable ML (Not a Black Box)

Every prediction comes with ranked SHAP explanations:
"Mortgage silent 12 days" • "Construction/mortgage out of sync"

Portal shows BOTH: ML confidence score AND real milestone completion per category. Users see why.

"We're telling you, you have a problem before you know you have a problem. Okay, great. Thank you."

Open Question Addressed

How we calculate preventable loss — the live equation.

Is there a running ticker showing what a builder is losing? Yes. Here's how it works in the code today.

The Formula (From Production Code)

Preventable Loss =
  Σ max(0, target_close − original_close)
    × $500/day

target_close_date: current projected close
original_close_date: date at contract signing
Positive diff = actual delay days (close date pushed back)

We use $500/day — conservative vs. industry data ($670/day, APB). Defensible in investor conversations.

Three pricing models. Start per-deal, convert to platform.

How much does it cost? Subscription or license? Here's our recommendation.

Option A: Per-Home Fee

$500–$2,000 per home transaction

Pro: Aligns cost with value. Easy ROI story.
Con: Revenue fluctuates with housing cycles.

Option B: Platform Subscription

Base fee + per-home fee

Pro: Guaranteed minimum + upside.
