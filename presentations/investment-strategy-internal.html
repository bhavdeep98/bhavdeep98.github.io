The Core Problem

Siloed systems show local status — not whether the home will close.

Fragmented truth

Construction, mortgage, title, CRM, and financial systems each hold one part of the transaction. No source contains the complete path to close.

Dependencies stay hidden

A home can look healthy inside one system while another team is stalled. Weekly meetings and spreadsheets discover the mismatch after risk has accumulated.

Action comes too late

Teams respond after dates move, incentives rise, or buyers cancel instead of intervening while the outcome can still change.

Enterprise Integration

Read-only architecture reduces operational risk; contracts still matter.

Technical boundary

Connector credentials are tenant-scoped. Egress controls permit reads, not operational write-back. Signals flow through ingestion, Kafka, the graph engine, PostgreSQL, API, and portal.

Commercial and legal boundary

Each deployment still requires customer authorization, source-system terms review, data-processing agreements, access controls, retention rules, and security review. We do not characterize integration as categorically barrier-free.

Construction

Schedule and milestone status from systems such as Procore and BuildPro.

Mortgage & title

Status signals, not underwriting, settlement, or lending decisions.

CRM & financial

Contract, close-date, and operational status needed for cross-system visibility.

Positioning: an authorized analytics layer above existing systems — never a replacement and never an autonomous actor inside builder tools.

Prediction Mechanics

Useful today; production learning must prove what generalizes.

Cross-system progress

Detect when construction, mortgage, and title are moving at incompatible rates. A home can look healthy inside one system and still be at risk across the full path to close.

Signal velocity and staleness

Measure recent activity and source freshness per deal. Silence is a feature to test, not a claim that it is universally the strongest predictor.

Neighborhood and builder baselines

Compare a home with similar communities, products, seasons, and lifecycle stages using the builder's own history where available.

Explainability and intervention

Rank contributing factors and pair them with a human-owned next action. Production data must validate calibration, precision, drift, and whether additional model mechanisms are needed.

Hiring implication: fund ML/platform engineering to review feature validity and retrain on real builder outcomes. Current simulated-data performance is product evidence, not proof of production ROI.

Illustrative division view

0 + 8 + 14 = 22 delayed days × $500 = $11,000 delay exposure. Annual scenario: 200 homes/month × 12% delayed × 35% preventable × 18 days × $500 × 12 = $907,200/year.

What $500/day is — and is not

An internal planning assumption, not a measured carrying cost, and it is configurable per builder. Pure carrying cost (financing, taxes, insurance, upkeep) is roughly $75–$205/day across a $250K–$800K home. Broader cost-of-delay references run higher — APB estimates ~$670/day on a $750K build. We set $500 deliberately between the two.

Key caveats: this is estimated exposure, not realized savings; the $500/day rate, the 12% delayed / 35% preventable / 18-day assumptions, and the exposure formula are all hypotheses to validate in a paid pilot before any outcome-based fee. A $700/day sensitivity raises the annual scenario to ~$1.27M.

Carrying-cost range is a bottom-up planning estimate (~9% of home value/year across financing, property tax, insurance, and upkeep), not a builder quote; financing rate is the main swing factor. Platform Expansion

Closing Confidence is the wedge; normalized cross-system data is the platform.

Now · Product Wedge

Protect the close

Rank homes by on-time-close probability, explain the cross-system risk, and route a human-owned next action before the date moves.

Next · Operating Intelligence

Improve the transaction

Use the same event history to identify cycle-time bottlenecks, cancellation patterns, stale handoffs, forecast variance, and recurring process failures.

Later · Portfolio Intelligence

Optimize the business

Benchmark divisions and communities, measure interventions, detect incentive leakage, and support planning with builder-specific historical patterns.

Connect systems → normalize events → predict risk → record action → measure outcome → improve the model

Why expansion is credible

New workflows reuse the connector network, home identity, transaction graph, event history, and outcome labels already required by Closing Confidence.

Why expansion is not in the base case

Customer willingness to pay, attributable value, and module priority must come from production pilots. These are growth options, not current revenue claims.

Platform thesis: begin with one urgent decision — which homes need attention today — then expand across the transaction using the data foundation already deployed.

Revenue Model Options

Test three models in paid pilots — do not present a pricing decision yet.

Option A · Usage-Led

$100 per monitored home

Charge once for each home monitored during the contract year, with an annual volume commitment and true-up.

Strength: simple, transparent, and scales with usage.
Risk: caps the base U.S. wedge TAM at $67.9M and varies with housing volume.

Option B · Platform + Volume

Annual platform subscription

Price by division or enterprise with an included annual home band, volume overages, and negotiated implementation terms.

Strength: budgetable revenue that reflects integration and platform value.
Risk: harder to set before ROI and adoption are proven.

Option C · Base + Outcome

Verified-outcome finding fee

Combine an annual minimum or per-home base with a capped share of value from qualifying findings that lead to verified outcomes.

Strength: aligns price with measurable customer value and creates upside.
Risk: requires agreed baselines, attribution, audit rights, timing, and fee caps.

What every paid pilot measures

Homes monitored, source freshness, prediction calibration, findings accepted, interventions completed, close-date movement, avoidable exposure, workflow adoption, and procurement response to each price structure.

Decision gate after 2–3 pilots

Choose the model that produces the clearest customer ROI, lowest attribution disputes, strongest gross margin, most predictable renewal, and easiest enterprise procurement — not the largest spreadsheet TAM.

Current modeling convention: TAM, SAM, SOM, and fundraising milestones use Option A at $100 per home only so the deck has one comparable base case. That is a modeling assumption, not a commercial decision.

Blended US/India engineering

A senior US lead paired with an India engineering pod cuts fully loaded engineering cost by roughly 40–55% versus US-only hiring, freeing about $200K into reserve and extending runway toward ~30 months at the same milestones.

Staging

At close: founder compensation, ML/platform review, product design. After first paid pilot: add integration/security capacity. After two pilots: release dedicated GTM hiring and larger event spend.

Offshore guardrails

Offshore access to customer data stays inside tenant-scoped controls, DPAs, and least-privilege roles; pilots use synthetic or de-identified data until enterprise security review clears production access.

Operating principle: these are maximum allocations, not instructions to spend every dollar. The India hub lowers burn per unit of engineering output, so capacity and runway scale further without a larger raise.

Why cost stays low

A national builder runs tens of thousands of active homes, not millions. Live cost is a fixed high-availability baseline plus a small streaming/storage increment — roughly 0.1% of a $100/home contract at national scale.

Why history is an asset, not a burden

Completed homes move once into S3/Parquet at ~$20–75/month and become labeled training data and portfolio benchmarks. Cheap to keep, compounding in value — the data foundation behind the platform-expansion story.

Appendix · Optional Direction

Blockchain is an option, not a product dependency.

Position

Omit it from the core investor thesis and near-term roadmap. Confidence scoring, escalation, and the audit log work without a distributed ledger. Revisit only if several customers or title/recording authorities request shared verification.

Market pull is the trigger. There is no arbitrary two- or three-year restart date.

Three different legal questions

Private audit trail: ordinary enterprise data-provenance use; not inherently a security.

Blockchain-recorded deed: subject to state property, notarization, filing-format, and local recording procedures.

Fractional investment token: securities law may apply when the economic arrangement is an investment contract; changing the record format does not avoid that analysis.

Sources: federal E-SIGN statute; Arizona blockchain statute; SEC digital-asset investment-contract framework. State and local rules remain transaction-specific; obtain counsel before deployment.
